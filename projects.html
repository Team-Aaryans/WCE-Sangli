The Rabbit Hole Detector

The Rabbit Hole Detector is a Google Chrome extension developed during the Angel Hacks Hackathon by a team of Memphis Hackclubbers. It is designed to make Chrome users more productive with time by visually showing them their website usage with pie charts. The extension stops people from going down a rabbit hole on certain websites by kicking them off after a period of time. It comes with a predesignated list of sites, but users can also add their own.

Bestern Western

The Bestern Weastern is a Shooting gallery type game where you have to gun down crimales and spare the innocent. As the game progresses the timing gets faster and faster so you have to make split decisions to continue further. The Bestern Weastern challenges your reflexes and quick thinking to achieve a new high score, do you have what it takes?

Want To Help The Earth?

Philip Pounds, Wenchi Liu, Jackson Preisler, and Ben Fowler all collaborated to make Want To Help The Earth?, an app for people to arrange local clean ups in their area to clean up the environment. We believe the power of many small clean ups can have a big impact on preserving our world, and therefore wanted to make it easier to do.

Japanese Flashcards

Arielle made the Japanese Flashcards,which simply provides numerous tips and tricks to the Japanese language, and includes many flashcards to help study kanji, hiragana, katakana, vocabulary, verbs, phrases, and adjectives. It also has the option of creating customized flashcards. Also written in are tips and tricks, definitions for various categories, so that the learner always has a reference.

Zoom Bookmarks

Zoom Bookmarks is a Chrome extension developed by Blake and Philip to be used as a workshop in Memphis Hack Club. This extension worked so well, we decided to list it on the Chrome Store. The Zoom Bookmarks allows users to save meeting info for recurring zoom meetings. You can download Zoom Bookmarks on the Chrome Store
